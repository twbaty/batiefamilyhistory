<h2>About This Blog</h2>

<p>The <em>Batie Family History</em> Second Edition — a multi-volume genealogical record compiled by Howard F. BATIE — traces the descendants of Thomas BATIE (c.&thinsp;1720–d.&thinsp;before 1806) and his wife Margaret ROBSON (1724–1806) from their origins on the England-Scotland border through their emigration to Ontario, Canada, and beyond.</p>

<p>This blog was created to serve as a living companion to that book. The publisher no longer holds the master copy (the photographs were returned to the original contributors years ago), and no additional print runs are planned. These posts provide out-of-band updates on individual family members — new records found, newly identified causes of death, military service, local histories — things that came to light after the book went to press and deserve a place in the family record.</p>

<hr>

<h2>About the Book</h2>

<p>The <strong>First Edition</strong> of the <em>Batie Family History</em> was published in 1991 and focused on the descendants of Adam BATIE (1799–1856) and Mary ROBSON (1803–1871). The <strong>Second Edition</strong> reaches two generations further back, beginning with Thomas BATIE and Margaret ROBSON. Though it carries the BATIE name, it is equally a ROBSON family history and a TELFER family history — these families lived in close proximity in Northumberland County, England, and emigrated together to the same community known as the "English Settlement" near London, Ontario. Chief among the related lines covered are the TELFER, BATY, HERDMAN, GRIEVE, CHARLTON, ORD, and SCOTT families. Several hundred direct descendants still reside in the London, ON area today.</p>

<p>The Second Edition draws on church and civil records from Northumberland County dating from the mid-1700s, land and county records from New York State and Middlesex County, Ontario from 1819 onward, and contributions from family members across nearly every branch of the expanded family tree. Where conflicting information was found, additional independent research was conducted to reconcile it.</p>

<hr>

<h2>Our Origins</h2>

<p>The earliest known record of the BATIE surname appears in the England-Scotland border region of Northumberland County, England, in the late 1600s — specifically at the Falstone Presbyterian Chapel in the valley of the North Tyne River, an area then known as North Tynedale. The same records show the ROBSON and TELFER families living in close proximity.</p>

<p>The BATIE surname is of Old English or Saxon origin; its most common English variant is BATY. Both spellings are found across English and Scottish counties, predominantly in Northumberland. The name is most likely derived from the Christian name Bartholomew. In Scotland, the similar name BEATTIE/BEATTY is found, though no known connection to the BATIEs has been established.</p>

<p>The earliest known record of the BATIE surname in the Falstone Register of Marriages is the 1751 marriage of our family progenitor:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>"July 9 1751 Thomas Baty and Margaret Robson both of this Chapelry Married by Banns."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Thomas and Margaret had four children, all baptized at Falstone Presbyterian Chapel: Margaret (1752), Elizabeth (1754), Arthur (1758), and John (1762). By 1824, all known descendants of Thomas and Margaret — with the exception of their eldest daughter Margaret, who married William CHARLTON — had emigrated from Northumberland County, England to London Township, Ontario. Their descendants eventually spread to Wisconsin, New York State, and across North America.</p>

<p>The TELFER line entered the family when Thomas and Margaret's second child, Elizabeth BATIE (1754–1802), married Robert TELFER in 1779. Robert later emigrated to Ontario in 1819 with his son Thomas TELFER; he is buried in what is now Telfer Cemetery near Ilderton, Ontario. The ROBSON line extends through multiple intermarriages across generations.</p>

<h2>The Editor</h2>

<p>The <em>Batie Family History</em> was compiled and edited by <strong>Howard F. BATIE</strong> (CGHCCA), who also served as editor of the <em>Batie Family Lettergram</em>, a semi-annual newsletter that carried family updates between editions. Howard credits the book's real authors as the hundreds of family members who responded to questionnaires, shared family Bible inscriptions, and contributed photographs — "It is all of you who have really authored this Family History by providing the detailed information which I have but edited and molded into a hopefully entertaining story of our family."</p>

<p>Research assistance came from professional genealogist J. Kenneth BROWN of Newcastle-Upon-Tyne and from serious researchers across nearly every branch of the family, including J. Russell BATIE (Lincoln, NE), W. Garfield and Ed BOLAND (Ottawa and Belmont, ON), Jean T. Evans (Hale Center, TX), and many others.</p>
