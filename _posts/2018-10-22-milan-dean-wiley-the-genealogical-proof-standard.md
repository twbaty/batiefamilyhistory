Errors in family history information happen all the time.&nbsp; There is a standard for collecting genealogical information.&nbsp; It's called the Genealogical Proof Standard.<br />
<br />
The Genealogical Proof Standard has five elements:
<br />
<ul>
<li>a reasonably exhaustive research;</li>
<li>complete and accurate source citations;</li>
<li>analysis and correlation of the collected information;</li>
<li>resolution of any conflicting evidence; and</li>
<li>a soundly reasoned, coherently written conclusion.</li>
</ul>
<br />
The aim is to provide direction on collecting accurate data for your genealogical research.<br />
<br />
Milan Dean Wiley is a good study on this standard.<br />
<br />
Milan was born 30 Nov 1925 in Chetopa, Kansas.&nbsp; I have reference to several documents to verify the date.&nbsp; Of most use was his WWII Draft Registration.&nbsp; This is a document he signed - making this a contemporaneous document.&nbsp; That is, we can infer the accuracy of the document by the fact he signed it.&nbsp; This is a much better source for a date of birth than say an obituary.<br />
<br />
Next we have the 1930 and 1940 Census records.&nbsp; These records tell us he was in Kansas during those years.<br />
<br />
I show Milan married Betty Virginia Miller on 11 Mar 1946.&nbsp; I found this in Betty's obituary.&nbsp; Since I don't have another source conflicting or supporting this information, I am left to believe the data.<br />
<br />
I also show that Milan passed away 1 July 1991.&nbsp; This is demonstrated by Social Security Death Index.&nbsp; Betty's obituary says he passed away in 1991.<br />
<br />
Then I ran into a perplexing situation.&nbsp; Milan's headstone.&nbsp; His headstone has him listed as Milan Dean Willy.<br />
<br />
Willy?&nbsp; The date of birth and death are accurate for Milan.&nbsp; The location is appropriate - Oswego, Kansas.&nbsp; According to <a href="http://www.findagrave.com/" target="_blank">FindAGrave.com</a>, Milan and Betty are both buried in Oswego Cemetery.&nbsp; I would expect they would have plots beside each other.&nbsp; That would suggest similar headstones.&nbsp; In this case, they aren't similar.&nbsp; And Betty's headstone shows her to be Betty Virginia Wiley - adding more confusion to the situation.<br />
<br />
Now, I need to make a visit to Oswego Cemetery to see just where each of their plots are.&nbsp; And is Milan near other's named Willy? <br />
