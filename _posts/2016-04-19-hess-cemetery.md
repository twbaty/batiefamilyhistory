This cemetery located in section #34 in Harrison Township, on the Cadiz
 Pike just north-west of the town of Cadiz. It was established sometime 
in the mid 1830s when Tabor and Sarah Mckee deeded 1 1/2 acres to John P
 Cooper to be used as a "Graveyard" with access to the cemetery from the
 Lewisville and Middletown Pike, which runs east of the graveyard. Tabor
 Mckee and his wife Sarah had purchased the land in 1830 and later sold 
it to Milton Hess in March of 1853, excepting the graveyard, which they 
retained possession of. In July of 1867, Tabor and Sarah Mckee deeded 
the cemetery to the Henry county Board of Commissioners', which in 
February of 1907 deeded the cemetery to the Harrison Township trustee. 
The cemetery got its name "Hess" from the cemetery being on the old Hess
 farm.

 stones were damaged by the tornado of 1974. Occasionally cattle get 
into the cemetery but luckily no damage has occurred. The cemetery is 
well maintained by the township trustee and it is still used 
occasionally for burials. Several stones need reset and cleaned, 
otherwise it is in good shape.
