<div style="color: #222222; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
I was rereading some of my finds and stumbled back across the attached...</div>
<div style="color: #222222; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
<br /></div>
<div style="color: #222222; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
I initially found this document related to Acree Clyde. &nbsp;</div>
<div style="color: #222222; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
<br /></div>
<div style="color: #222222; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
"...vs "Battling" (Acree) Franklin, a promising contender of College avenue."</div>
<div style="color: #222222; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
<br /></div>
<div style="color: #222222; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
What do you know, we have a pugilist in the family! &nbsp;Then I continued to read and lo and behold, we had 2 pugilists in the family! &nbsp;</div>
<div style="color: #222222; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
<br /></div>
<div style="color: #222222; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
"Lightening" &nbsp;(Ernest) Franklin, champion of College avenue..."<br />
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Now the confusing part. &nbsp;in the first paragraph is the statement "...Danville youths..." &nbsp;This would lead to one of three things:</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
1. The youth of the time were extremely into boxing.</div>
<div>
2. The boxing on the card was being done by "youth".</div>
<div>
3. A combination of 1 and 2.</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
So, following this path, it would appear on one hand the "youth" were fighting. &nbsp;The article is 1923 which would make Ernest Franklin 10. &nbsp;On the other hand, it also makes Acree 42 - not exactly a youth any longer.<br />
<br />
At the end of it all, it would seem we are a boxing family!</div>
