Russell Jefferson Sage, my 3rd great uncle. b. 16 Feb 1884, d. 6 Jul 1926<br />
<br />
Russell was the Sheriff of Billings, Montana.&nbsp; By all accounts, Russell was a popular sheriff in Billings.&nbsp; On July 5, 1926, John Vickery was arrested and jailed.<br />
<br />
The story continues that Vickery was not well searched for weapons and was able to secret a pistol into the jail cell.&nbsp; The morning of July 6, 1926, Vickery was supposed to be put on a train destined for Kansas City, but the train was missed.&nbsp; Vickery was advised he would have to stay in jail until the next train.&nbsp; Vickery asked the deputy for some ice.&nbsp; When the deputy handed Vickery ice, and turned to leave, Vickery pulled a gun on the deputy and demanded to be released, and started shooting.&nbsp; Faking being hit, Vickery fell claiming to have been hit by a bullet.<br />
<br />
Russell alerted to the sound of gunfire and made his way to the jail.&nbsp; When he arrived, he found Vickery fallen.&nbsp; When Russell entered the cell, Vickery started shooting again, hitting Russell and killing him.<br />
<br />
Vickery escaped on horseback but was quickly apprehended.<br />
