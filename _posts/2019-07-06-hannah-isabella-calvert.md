<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">Hannah Isabella Calvert, born July 8, 1851 in Ontario Canada.&nbsp; Hannah married William Shipley Robson on July 5, 1882.&nbsp; They lived in London, Ontario, Canada all their days, the best I can tell.</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">William died young, 32 years old leaving Hannah a widow.&nbsp; Based on the Return of Deaths for Huron County, Wm. S Robson died April 26, 1887 by "killed by log".&nbsp; I'm unclear on why William was in Michigan.&nbsp; Especially that the majority of the family were farmers, it's uncertain why William was logging.&nbsp; If some family member has the story for this, please do let me know.</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<br /></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">Hannah didn't remarry and lived another 29 years.&nbsp; Her headstone lists her date of death as&nbsp;</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<br /></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">February <b>27</b>, 1916.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<br /></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">The official record lists her date of death as&nbsp;</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<br /></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">February <b>24</b>, 1916.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<br /></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">And incidentally, her cause of death was "apoplexy".</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">What caused the discrepancy in dates?&nbsp; A few hypothesis'.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">1. Since the headstone was carved after she passed away, the 27th was mistakenly noted as it was her date of burial.</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">2. The date of death was noted on her death record by the physician.</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;"></span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<br /></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">Comparing the date on the headstone with the date on the death record, it's reasonable to assume the death record is the more accurate of the two. So, Hannah's proper date of death is</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle" style="text-align: center;">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">February 24, 1916.</span></div>
<div class="userCardTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;"><br /></span></div>

<span style="font-weight: normal;"><br /></span></div>
