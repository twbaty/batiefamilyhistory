<p>Ralph Leslie Elliott was born <span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthDate">October 26, 1924 in</span> <span class="birthPlace">Corunna, Lambton County, Ont.&nbsp; He is my 4th Cousin twice removed.&nbsp; The son of Thomas Fleming Elliott and Mary Christina Miller.
</span></span></p>

<p><span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace">I don't have any details about Ralph's life prior to joining the Royal Air Force in 1943.</span></span></p>

<p><span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace">While in the RAF, Ralph earned the Air Bomber Badge in May 1944. 
</span></span></p>

<p><span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace">He progressed in rank from</span></span></p>

<p><span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace"><span>&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;</span>Aircraftman Second Class (AC2)
<span>&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;</span>Leading Aircraftman (LAC)

<p><span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace">On December 6, 1944, while stationed in at RAF Station Wigtown in southwest Scotland.&nbsp; He was walking with a friend on the camp road to the Air Crew Dressing room prior to reporting for duty to the Briefing Room.&nbsp; A vehicle struck Ralph from behind causing what would end up being fatal injuries.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></span></p>
<span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace">The driver wasn't given the authority to drive the vehicle that struck Ralph and his friend.&nbsp;</span></span>
<p><span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace">Ralph was immediately taken to E.M.S. Hospital, at the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galloway_House" target="_blank">Galloway House</a>&nbsp; with severe injuries.&nbsp; His injuries were listed as:</span></span></p>

<p><span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace"><u>Injuries</u> Fracture of skull - bleeding from nostrils and contusion and hemorrhage around right orbit.&nbsp; Lacerated wound of left cheek. abrasions to scalp.</span></span></p>

<p><span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace"><u>Limbs</u> Rt. limb displaced so that foot was over Lt. shoulder; fracture of lower end of rt. femur found.&nbsp; Lt. leg - posterior dislocation of knee joint with fracture of patella and deep laceration wound into knee joint, abrasions of both legs, and abrasions of both hands.</span></span></p>

<p><span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace">Ralph was holding strong into the night.&nbsp; So much so, his injuries were addressed; "fractures and dislocations reduced", "lacerations cleaned and sutured".&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></span></p>

<p><span class="userCardEventDetail factBirth"><span class="birthPlace">Approximately 5:00 A.M. on December 7th, Ralph was listed as "suddenly condition deteriorated and patient died".
</span></span></p>
