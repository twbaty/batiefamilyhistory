I learned something interesting the other day.  I try to take photos for <a href="http://findagrave.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">http://findagrave.com/</a>
 requests where I can (shameless plug!).  This request had me looking 
for something I had never heard of - a columbarium.  Turns out I had 
seen them, been to them, just never knew what they were.  And this one, 
was inside an Episcopal Church.

After researching this more, All Souls 
Episcopal Church in OKC has this explanation:

<blockquote class="tr_bq">
<i>Columbarium:  It is an ancient practice to bury the dead<span class="text_exposed_show">
 within the church and the Columbarium provides a way of doing this.  
...the Columbarium consists of closed niches for the repose of the 
cremated remains of the departed.  It also reminds us of the Communion 
of Saints, living and dead.</span></i></blockquote>

<div class="text_exposed_show">

St. John Neumann Catholic Church, TX continues with

<blockquote class="tr_bq">
<i>It is an 18th century Latin word for dovecote or dove nest.  Today, it 
is a place where urns holding cremains (ashes) may be inurned (reposed) 
in individual niches.</i></blockquote>

So, I'm one more word smarter today!
