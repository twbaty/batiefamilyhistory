<h4 class="persNameTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">Annie Robson Douglas, 1858-1897.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></h4>

<h4 class="persNameTitle">

<span style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp;Daughter of Thomas and Cicely (Scott) Douglas.&nbsp; Married John Turnbull, 7 Dec 1892. </span></h4>

<h4 class="persNameTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">Again, another yet unseen and unclear cause of death.&nbsp; </span></h4>

<h4 class="persNameTitle">

<i><span style="font-weight: normal;">Puerperal Convulsions</span></i></h4>

<h4 class="persNameTitle">

<span style="font-weight: normal;">Whatever the certainty of this particular term, it had to be an unpleasant event for the woman, based on readings below.&nbsp; I'm not finding a reference to children for John Turnbull, her husband.&nbsp; I'm left to conclude that Annie and child both passed away in childbirth.</span></h4>

<h4 class="persNameTitle">

<span style="font-weight: normal;"> </span></h4>

<h4 class="persNameTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">I'm having trouble finding credible and consistent definitions of this phenomenon. &nbsp; And, I'm mostly finding definitions of what it is from the 1850's.&nbsp; Which makes sense with Annie's date of death.</span></h4>

<h4 class="persNameTitle">
<span style="font-weight: normal;">
<blockquote>
<i>One thing at least seems certain.&nbsp; In the second half of the 19th century childbirth was believed, by alienists as well as obstetricians,to be a common cause of a form of insanity which was usually manic, often severe and occasionally fatal.&nbsp; It is seldom mentioned in 18th century obstetric texts&nbsp; and first appears in the 1820s and 1830s 5•&nbsp; Only in later obstetric texts do we find that puerperal insanity is granted the status of a common and important complication of obstetric care with which the student was expected to be familiar6•s.&nbsp; In this century,&nbsp; however,&nbsp; as obstetrics became increasingly surgical, puerperal insanity drifted away from obstetrics into the growing specialty of psychiatry.</i>

<i>
