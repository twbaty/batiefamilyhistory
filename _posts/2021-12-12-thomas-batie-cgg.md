<p>&nbsp;Thomas <b>BATIE </b>(CGG) was born in 1835 in London, Middlesex, Ontario, Canada.&nbsp; Thomas married&nbsp;Letitia <b>WILDMAN</b>, daughter of James and Margaret WILDMAN.</p>

<p>Thomas and Letitia had 5 children</p>

<p><span>&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;</span>William Fletcher <b>BATIE </b>(CGGA) (1861-1928)</p>

<p><span>&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;</span>Henry Adam <b>BATIE </b>(CGGB) (1864-1952)</p>

<p><span>&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;</span>Adam Robson <b>BATIE </b>(CGGC) (1867-1934)</p>

<p><span>&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;</span>Margaret Rankin <b>BATIE </b>(CGGD) (1868-1940)</p>

<p><span>&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;</span>Eliza Mary <b>BATIE </b>(CGGE) (1871-1955)</p>

<p>A few things are documented about Thomas <b>BATIE </b>in the book, <i><a href="https://cdn.website-editor.net/020d9c979f77483189db333592c7de7f/files/uploaded/History%2520of%2520Sac%2520County%252C%2520Iowa%2520%25281914%2529.pdf" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">History of Sac County, Iowa</a></i> by William H Hart.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>Thomas was a charter member of the Laurel Lodge No 517, Ancient Free and Accepted Masons in 1891.</p>

<p>Eliza, his daughter, married James Wilbur <b>NEAL</b>, a strong and influential citizen of Sac county, Iowa.</p>

<p>Thomas was also at the first meeting of the organization of The Sac County Farmers Mutual Fire Insurance Association in 1876.&nbsp; He was elected as a director.</p>

<p>Thomas was Sheriff three different times.&nbsp; The book chronicles two of the votes.</p>
