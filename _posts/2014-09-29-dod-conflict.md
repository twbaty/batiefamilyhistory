William Walter Anderson 

I am researching four different trees; my dads, Batie; my moms, Franklin, my wife's mom's, Hale and my wife's dad's, Thornton.&nbsp; On occasion I will post something about one of these other trees. While they become "Batie" trees as I'm now related to them, strictly speaking they aren't I suppose.&nbsp; But, the nature of this blog supersedes strict adherence to protocol.

William Walter Anderson.

The husband of the 1st cousin of the husband of the aunt of my wife.&nbsp; OK, I got way offline on this one.&nbsp; But I'm glad I did - it's an interesting find.&nbsp; At least to me... 

William has conflicting dates of birth. 

WWI Draft Registration - DOB 13 Jul 1895

WWII Draft Registration - DOB 13 Jul 1895

Military Headstone Application - DOB 13 Jul 1895

Death Certificate - DOB 13 Jul 1892

I've been taught to trust the Death Certificate.&nbsp; And it seems reasonable to trust one that has both parents listed accurately.&nbsp; However, after looking at the other items, the death certificate is the only one that wasn't "signed off on" by William or a relative.&nbsp; It may have been created after interviewing family members, but it doesn't have their seal of approval.

For now, I'm going to have to trust the three documents that were signed off on by William or family.

Note:&nbsp; It is very interesting to me that William died at Fort Sam Houston - an Army fort.&nbsp; The military.&nbsp; The government.&nbsp; The informant on the death certificate is listed as "Official Records, Brooke Army Hospital, Fort Sam Houston, Texas".&nbsp; Effectively the same organization that was provided the other three documents.&nbsp; One would think given the first three items listed above, the fourth item would be accurate.&nbsp; I suppose that is government efficiency.&nbsp;
