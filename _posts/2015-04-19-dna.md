<br />
Growing up I was told I had Native American (Indian) ancestry. &nbsp;It wasn't hard to believe. &nbsp;I tanned VERY well. &nbsp;Everyone I knew in my family (OK, mostly my dads side) was from in or around Oklahoma. &nbsp;Growing up I recall being inundated with headdresses, spears, bow and arrows.&nbsp; I liked alll the archetypal Indian paraphernalia. <br />
<br />
As well, I kept being told I was Black Dutch. &nbsp;I'm not really sure what that is - it's neither black (African American) nor Dutch. &nbsp;In a quick search on the Internet, I read that it could refer to several ethnicities, but there seems to not be a universal definition of the term.<br />
<br />
<br />
A few years back I wanted to take a DNA test to find out my actual breakdown.&nbsp; I really thought I would show native American. &nbsp;I assumed some English or Scottish. &nbsp;I wasn't sure what my mother's side of the family would bring.<br />
<br />
I was given a DNA test for my birthday! &nbsp;It was different than I thought it would be. &nbsp;Watching the CSI shows on TV - they come in with a buccal swab, wipe the perp's cheek, and within about 15 minutes they know the person is a suspect or not. &nbsp;No such luck in real life!<br />
<br />
First off, there was no swab. &nbsp;But there was a vial. &nbsp;I had to fill the vial with spit. &nbsp;Not gross - but odd. &nbsp;You don't realize it, but you dry out if all you're doing is spitting. &nbsp;It took a little while to rewet. <br />
<br />
There was a padded package provided to mail the spit vial in to keep it protected. &nbsp;As well, the vial was barcoded allowing me to track my spit - at least confirm the testing site's receipt of my vial and monitor for when the test was complete.<br />
<br />
Then there was the 6 week wait. &nbsp;What happened to 15 minutes? Guess that's just TV magic. &nbsp;Six weeks later, my results were available.<br />
<br />
Well... &nbsp;The results were in. &nbsp;Not what I expected to see, but it's an answer.<br />
<br />
100% British Isles<br />
