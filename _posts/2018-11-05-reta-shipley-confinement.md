Reta Shipley was born July 2, 1897.&nbsp; She passed away March 15, 1922.&nbsp; Information I had collected by all accounts back in 2015.&nbsp; She is the wife of my 3rd Cousin 3x Removed, George Meridith Charlton.&nbsp; I had also collected the information that her son, Morris Shipley Charlton was born and passed away on the same day - March 15, 1922.<br />
<br />
My usual question is "Why?".&nbsp; Why did they move there?&nbsp; Why did he take that job?&nbsp; Why did they open that shop?&nbsp; Why didn't they do something different?&nbsp; Lots of 'why's out there.<br />
<br />
Which confuses me more that I didn't ask why did Reta and Morris pass away on the same day.<br />
<br />
To which I'm appreciative of another Ancestry.com user,&nbsp;<span style="background-color: white; color: #333333;"><a href="https://www.ancestry.com/connect/profile/035f340e-0006-0000-0000-000000000000" target="_blank">Jill Creighton Roberts</a></span><span style="background-color: white; color: #333333; font-family: &quot;source sans pro&quot; , &quot;helvetica neue&quot; , &quot;arial&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 16px;">,&nbsp;</span>&nbsp;that steered me to the answer of "Why?" before I realized I needed to ask it!<br />
<br />
Jill pointed out that Reta's cause of death was listed as "confinement".&nbsp; Thankfully, Jill also pointed out that confinement is an old term for childbirth.&nbsp; Being the naturally curious sort, I looked up confinement.&nbsp; To which I found three potential meanings thru the years.<br />
<br />
<br />
Estimated date of confinement - simply put, this is the expected due date.&nbsp; EDC for short.<br />
<br />
Prenatal confinement - in the early Victorian era, it was not unusual to confine the mother to bed rest for a period of time.&nbsp; Generally the last trimester.<br />
<br />
Postpartum confinement - some customs feel the mother is weak after childbirth and need a period of confinement for the mother to recover from childbirth and care for the newborn child.<br />
<br />
Reta was most likely in that period of prenatal confinement.<br />
