OK, while not my Batie family, I have a story about my mom's dad.

I never knew my grandfather - he has never been "grandpa", "grand-dad" or even "hey you".&nbsp; He has always been "Julian Clyde Franklin" - Mom's dad.

I found an image of my grandfather - my mom's dad. He was sitting with his parents in front of an old, cathedral style building.&nbsp; I couldn't find any family that knew where this building was.&nbsp; Or why he was there with his parents.

On the back of the photograph, my mother wrote "Probably in the early 1930's".&nbsp; It wasn't much, but it was a start.&nbsp; In the early 1930's, Julian was in High Point, NC (according to Mom).&nbsp; I also noted that his obituary listed him as a lifelong Presbyterian.&nbsp; I started searching google for Presbyterian churches in High Point.&nbsp; I emailed a couple of churches and kept getting&nbsp; "You might try..." responses.&nbsp; This wasn't altogether discouraging - I was ruling out churches.&nbsp; According to Sherlock Holmes, “When you have eliminated the impossible, whatever remains, however improbable, must be the truth, "

But then, I ran out of churches in High Point.&nbsp; I was lost.

I took to google images.&nbsp; I forget the exact search I entered, but the second search (that much I remember!) about half way down the page, I found the location.&nbsp; The Duke University Chapel!!&nbsp; Wow - a search on google images with text words found a picture - that's just way too kewl!!!!!

Now, when was the photo taken?&nbsp; That's my next search.&nbsp; I've contacted a couple of places at Duke and so far I don't have a good answer.&nbsp; I'm keying in on the tree just over Julian's dad's left shoulder.&nbsp; When was that tree in that place?&nbsp; I've found photos when it /wasn't/ there - but not when it was first there.

Anyone have any idea as to the approximate date based on clues in the photo?
